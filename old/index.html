I am a fourth year Ph.D. student in College of Information and Computer Sciences at UMass Amherst advised by Benjamin Marlin. I earned my Bachelors (Hons.) and Masters in Electrical Engineering from Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur and was awarded the prestigious Institute Silver Medal.

My research interests can be broadly categorized into generative modeling and the adversarial robustness of deep learning models. In particular, I am interested in exploring deep learning methods specifically latent-variable models for solving the problem of missing data/irregular sampling in time series. This work is motivated by the analysis of physiological time series data in electronic health records, which are sparse, irregularly sampled, and multivariate. Modeling such time series with deep learning can lead to accurate predictive models for diagnosis and other applications in healthcare. I'm also interested in exploring and developing algorithms and methods for making deep learning safe and more robust.
