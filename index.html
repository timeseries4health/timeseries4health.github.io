Learning from Time Series for Health

Time-series data are ubiquitous in healthcare, spanning electronic health records, wearable devices, physiological signals, and population-level trends. While these data offer immense potential for machine learning to extract actionable insights, they also present unique challenges: noisy or missing labels, irregular sampling, distribution shifts, multimodal complexity, and stringent requirements for interpretability, fairness, and reproducibility. Despite recent advances—including foundation models, multimodal fusion with LLMs, and robust methods for handling missingness, critical gaps remain, particularly in translating innovations into real-world clinical practice due to regulatory, ethical, and systemic barriers.


This workshop unites researchers and practitioners working on time-series modeling across diverse healthcare domains, from wearables to hospital systems, to address shared challenges in learning from physiological data. Through keynote talks, panels, and interactive sessions, we aim to: (1) explore diverse applications of health time-series analytics, (2) foster dialogue between industry and academic leaders, and (3) engage the community in solving open problems—such as causality, deployability, and scalability—to accelerate impactful, clinically viable solutions.
